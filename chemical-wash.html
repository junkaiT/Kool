When do you need a chemical wash?

⚠️
Musty or stale smell from your aircon

Mould and bacteria inside the coil cause odours that a general service can't fully remove. A chemical wash flushes the coil with bio-enzyme solution.

🕐
ΔT still low after a general service

If your aircon isn't getting cold despite a recent service, the coil likely needs a deeper clean. Our ΔT measurement will confirm.

📅
Visible mould on the filter or coil

Mould spreads and circulates in the air your family breathes. A chemical wash removes it properly.

How is a chemical wash different from a general service? ›
A general service cleans accessible surfaces. A chemical wash involves removing the front panel and flushing the evaporator coil with bio-enzyme solution — deeper cleaning for musty smells, mould, or persistent low ΔT.
How long does a chemical wash take? ›
About 45–60 minutes per unit. The bio-enzyme solution needs time to work before rinsing.
Will it fix my aircon not being cold? ›
If low ΔT is caused by a dirty coil, yes. Our ΔT measurement before and after will show the improvement. If ΔT is still low after a wash, we'll recommend the right next step — no pressure.
How often should I do a chemical wash? ›
Once a year is typical for most Singapore homes, alongside quarterly general servicing. More frequently if you notice smells or reduced cooling.
